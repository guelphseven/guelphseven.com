The Apps

Well, we haven't made these yet!

Our applications may be developed as web services, mobile device applications, desktop apps, plugins, or APIs. We've all got cool ideas that we can't wait to try out, but they will have to be pragmatic enough that we can accomplish them (or a minimum visable product version of them) in a single day's work.

Each day, we'll come together to brainstorm ideas for what to make, and then we'll set out like madfolk, coding and designing for countless hours, converging several times as we iterate towards a Version 1 of our product. We'll stop only to inhale delicious morsels designed to keep us alert and healthy (and alive), and to support our other unfortunate bodily needs (looking at you, sleep.)

If you're bored and you'd just like to check out some cool apps, go here and see what the UWaterloo students made last time.

What's this all about?

In November of 2010, 7 Waterloo students worked for 7 days, to create 7 sweet apps and show off their skills. They enjoyed it so much that they're doing it again. We enjoyed the process and results so much that this time, we're joining them. Seven University of Guelph students will engage in friendly competition with a newly formed 7^3 dream team at Waterloo to make amazing applications.

When's it all happening?

From March 5th to 11th, we will be releasing one application per day. Leading up to that, we'll be posting short bios for each team member, performing a dry run, thanking sponsors profusely, and updating the world with our process. When the whole thing's over, we'll give a postmortem rundown and see what we've learned. Then we'll start to make plans for next time.

These can't be useful ideas!

Au contraire. Check out the original 7^3 site from November. There's tremendous utility there. Of course, we don't propose that we'll be making polished, perfect, never-ever-fail-on-your-life applications in a single day! We're going to follow cool thoughts through to completion and unleash awesome ideas onto the world, and then we can revisit whatever the world shows interest in, and flesh it out into an even better and more perfect product.

Cool; what else?

Follow us on twitter and watch our progress! Try out our apps as we release them, give us feedback by tweeting at us, blog about us, submit links to HackerNews and generally tell people about this! We're cool people and we'd like our names out there, and we're hoping our work finds success. More importantly though is this idea itself: taking some time away from whatever else you're doing just to create, to collaborate, and to grow as a developer and a designer is a fantastic exercise. We want to show people that they can pull off amazing things - they just have to get up and do them. Hopefully we'll have even more teams join in for another round in the future, and this crazy concept can spread like a TED idea.
